Midjourney Prompts Basics

At its core, a Midjourney prompt is a text description that tells the AI what to create. However, treating it as a simple sentence is a recipe for disappointment. The AI model analyzes every token in your string, weighing the importance of subjects, backgrounds, lighting conditions, and artistic styles. A basic prompt like "a cyberpunk city" will generate an image, but it will be random and unpredictable. To gain control, you must expand this into a detailed narrative. You need to specify the time of day, the weather, the type of camera lens, the rendering engine, and the specific artist styles you want to emulate. Think of your prompt as a creative brief you would give to a human illustrator; the more specific the brief, the closer the result matches your intent.

Furthermore, understanding the syntax of Midjourney is crucial for refining your midjourney prompts. The order of words matters significantly; terms placed at the beginning of the prompt carry more weight than those at the end. You can also use parameters like --ar for aspect ratio, --v for version control, and --stylize to determine how artistic the AI should be. By combining natural language descriptions with these technical commands, you create a robust instruction set that guides the neural network toward a specific aesthetic. This balance between descriptive prose and technical constraints is the foundation of effective prompt engineering.

Key: Always start your prompt with the main subject, followed by the medium or style, then the environment, and finally the lighting and technical parameters. This hierarchy ensures the AI prioritizes your core concept.

Step by Step

Creating the perfect image doesn't have to be a guessing game. Follow this logical workflow to construct high-quality prompts consistently.

  1. Step 1: Define your core subject clearly. Be specific about what object, person, or scene you want. Instead of "dog," try "a weathered greyhound wearing a vintage aviator jacket."
  2. Step 2: Add context and environment. Where is this subject located? Is it in a neon-lit alleyway, a sun-drenched meadow, or a sterile laboratory? Details here set the mood.
  3. Step 3: Specify the artistic style and medium. Do you want a 35mm photograph, an oil painting, a vector illustration, or a Unreal Engine 5 render? Reference specific artists or movements to guide the texture and composition.
  4. Step 4: Apply technical parameters and lighting. Add details about lighting (e.g., "volumetric lighting," "golden hour") and append command flags like --ar 16:9 or --q 2 to finalize the technical specs.

Common Mistakes

Even experienced designers often fall into traps that degrade the quality of their generated images. Being aware of these pitfalls can save you significant time and frustration.

  • Mistake 1: Using conflicting descriptors. If you ask for a "minimalist" image that is also "highly detailed" and "cluttered," the AI gets confused, resulting in muddy, incoherent visuals. Stick to a cohesive aesthetic theme.
  • Mistake 2: Ignoring negative prompting or over-complicating the sentence structure. While Midjourney doesn't use a strict "negative prompt" field like Stable Diffusion, phrasing matters. Avoid long, run-on sentences with too many conjunctions. Break your ideas into clear, comma-separated phrases to ensure each element is processed distinctly.

Pro Tips

Once you have mastered the basics, it is time to elevate your workflow with advanced techniques that give you an edge over other users.

Pro Tip: Utilize "prompt weighting" by adding :: followed by a number to emphasize specific elements. For example, "red roses::2 white lilies::1" tells the AI that the red roses are twice as important to the composition as the lilies, giving you precise control over the visual hierarchy.
