Common Mistakes

Even with the best tools, it is easy to fall into bad habits that can hinder your progress. Many athletes make the error of overcomplicating their initial setups or failing to update their routines as they improve. Here are two common pitfalls to avoid when using the organizer:

  • Mistake 1: Creating sequences that are too long and complex to remember, which leads to hesitation and broken rhythm during the actual workout. Why it fails: If you have to stop and look at the screen to remember the next move, you lose your flow and your heart rate drops, reducing the conditioning benefit of the session.
  • Mistake 2: Never updating your saved styles, resulting in stagnation. Why it fails: As your skills improve, your old combinations may become too easy or technically limiting; failing to drag in new, more challenging moves means you stop growing as a fighter.

Pro Tips

Pro Tip: Create "Modular Blocks" rather than full workouts. Instead of building one massive 10-round routine, build three distinct 3-round modules (e.g., Warm-up, Technical Focus, Burnout) and drag-and-drop them together dynamically depending on how much time you have that day.
