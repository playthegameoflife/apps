Common Mistakes

Even with powerful tools, human error can still dilute the quality of your results. Here are the most frequent pitfalls writers fall into when trying to enhance their prompts:

  • Mistake 1: Over-complicating the Context. Many writers think "more is better" and paste entire documents into the prompt. Why it fails: This overwhelms the model, causing it to lose focus on the primary instruction. Keep context concise and relevant to the specific task at hand.
  • Mistake 2: Ignoring Negative Constraints. Failing to tell the AI what not to do. Why it fails: AI models are eager to please and will default to common tropes if you don't explicitly forbid them. Always include constraints like "Do not use buzzwords" or "Avoid introductory fluff."

Pro Tips

Pro Tip: To get truly unique responses, try the "Persona + Constraint" method. Instead of just saying "write like an expert," tell the AI: "Act as a weary but hopeful industry veteran who has seen it all. Use short, punchy sentences. Do not use the words 'leverage,' 'landscape,' or 'game-changer'." Specific negative constraints force the AI to search deeper into its vocabulary, resulting in fresher, more unique phrasing.
