If you tell this friend to "go to the store," they might walk to the nearest one without knowing what to buy or how much to spend. Common Mistakes

Even with the best tools, human error can still creep in. Understanding where others go wrong can help you avoid these pitfalls and get better results faster. Here are two frequent errors that degrade the quality of AI interactions:

  • Mistake 1: Being too vague or broad in your request. When you ask an AI to "write something about marketing," it doesn't know if you need a tweet, a whitepaper, or a slogan, leading to generic and unusable content.
  • Mistake 2: Forgetting to provide context or examples. AI models cannot read your mind; if you don't tell it who the audience is or provide a sample of the style you like, the output will lack the specific nuance you need.
