Common Mistakes

Even with the best tools, errors in execution can hinder your progress. Here are the two most common pitfalls fighters encounter when using randomization tools:

  • Mistake 1: Sacrificing form for speed. When the generator calls out a complex sequence quickly, many fighters panic and start sloppily throwing punches without rotating their hips or guarding their chin. This reinforces bad habits. Always prioritize clean technique over keeping up with the pace; you can always slow the timer down.
  • Mistake 2: Ignoring footwork and range. A common error is planting your feet and treating the generator like a static bag drill. In a real fight, you are moving. Failing to pivot, angle off, or manage distance while executing these random combos means you aren't training for reality. Always move your feet as if an opponent is countering you.
