Mastering Midjourney Prompts: A Practical Foundation

Before diving into specific techniques, you need to understand the core components that make midjourney prompts work. Midjourney interprets your text description and transforms it into visual output using sophisticated language models. The quality of your prompts directly determines the quality of your results.

Essential Prompt Components

  • Subject Description – Clearly identify what you want to depict. "A modernist chair" tells Midjourney exactly what to generate, while "furniture thing" produces unpredictable results.
  • Style Modifiers – Specify artistic styles, periods, or techniques. Examples include "impressionist oil painting," "Art Deco poster," or "minimalist vector illustration."
  • Technical Parameters – These control output format and quality. Parameters like --ar 16:9 for aspect ratio or --q 2 for higher quality give you control over the final deliverable.
  • Environmental Context – Where does your subject exist? "A modernist chair in a sunlit Scandinavian living room" provides context that "modernist chair" alone cannot.

Prompt Structure That Works

Effective midjourney prompts follow a logical structure: start broad with your main subject, add specific details and style descriptors, then include technical parameters. Consider this example comparing a weak prompt with a strong one:

Weak Prompt: "cat"
Strong Prompt: "A ginger tabby cat with emerald eyes, lounging on a vintage leather armchair, soft Rembrandt lighting, editorial photography style, shallow depth of field --ar 3:2 --q 2 --v 5"

The difference in output quality is dramatic. The strong prompt provides Midjourney with enough specific information to generate precisely what the designer envisions.

Understanding these fundamentals gives you the foundation to build increasingly sophisticated prompts. Now let's move into actionable steps you can implement right away.

How to Generate Midjourney Prompts: Step-by-Step

Follow this systematic process to create effective midjourney prompts consistently. Each step builds upon the previous, ensuring you develop prompts that deliver professional results.

Step 1: Define Your Visual Goal

Before typing anything, clearly articulate what you're trying to achieve. Are you creating a hero image for a website? Generating concept art for a product redesign? Finding inspiration for a branding project? Write down three to five specific attributes your ideal image must have. This clarity guides every decision you'll make in subsequent steps.

Step 2: Identify Core Subject Elements

Determine the primary subject of your image and any supporting elements. Be specific rather than generic. Instead of "building," consider "art deco skyscraper with gold leaf detailing." Instead of "person," think "senior architect in late sixties wearing wire-frame glasses." Specificity eliminates Midjourney's interpretive guesswork.

Step 3: Select Style and Mood

Choose an artistic style that aligns with your project requirements. Midjourney responds to hundreds of style references. Common categories include:

  • Artistic movements: Impressionism, Surrealism, Bauhaus
  • Medium types: Watercolor, charcoal sketch, digital illustration
  • Era references: 1920s Art Deco, Victorian era, retro 1980s
  • Designer names: Pixar style, Wes Anderson cinematography, Saul Bass graphic design

Step 4: Add Technical Parameters

Configure your prompt with parameters that control the output. Essential parameters include:

  • --ar [width:height] – Sets aspect ratio (16:9 for web banners, 1:1 for social posts, 9:16 for mobile)
  • --q [value] – Quality setting (0.25 to 2, higher equals better quality but slower processing)
  • --s [value] – Stylize parameter (100 to 1000, higher values add more artistic interpretation)
  • --v [version] – Model version (5, 5.1, 5.2 are current options)

Step 5: Test and Iterate

Generate your initial image, then evaluate critically. Does it match your vision? Identify specific discrepancies. Perhaps the lighting needs adjustment, or the composition feels off. Modify your prompt incrementally—change one element at a time to understand what produces different results. This iterative process refines your approach until you achieve the desired output.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake 1: Using overly complex prompts with contradictory elements. Midjourney struggles when asked to combine incompatible styles or subjects. If your prompt requires explanation, simplify it.

Mistake 2: Neglecting aspect ratio parameters. An image generated without --ar might produce unwanted dimensions, requiring time-consuming post-processing.

Mistake 3: Expecting perfect text generation within images. Midjourney handles typography poorly. If you need text, generate the image separately and add text using design software.
