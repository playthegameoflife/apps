Common Mistakes Freelancers Make with Business Proposals

  • Generic copy that screams template - Starting with "I am a freelance writer with 10 years experience" instead of addressing the client's specific situation
  • Vague deliverables without specifics - Saying "create engaging content" instead of "write 8 blog posts of 1500 words each"
  • No clear timeline or milestones - Missing delivery dates that show progress and accountability
  • Missing social proof or credentials - Not including relevant results from similar projects
  • Weak or missing call to action - Ending with "let me know if interested" instead of "I will send the contract today"
  • No pricing transparency - Hiding costs that should be upfront creates anxiety
  • Ignoring ATS systems - Many clients use Applicant Tracking Systems - you need relevant keywords in your proposal

Frequently Asked Questions

How long should my proposal be?

Keep it to 2-3 pages maximum. Include a one-page executive summary, followed by detailed scope, timeline, and pricing. Anything longer gets skimmed or deleted.

Should I include pricing upfront?

Yes, vague pricing creates anxiety and often gets you filtered out. Include ballpark pricing in the summary, with detailed breakdown later. Clients respect transparency.

How do I stand out from other freelancers?

Show specific results from similar past work - numbers matter. "Increased conversions by 47%" speaks louder than "I am a great writer." Proof beats promises every time.

What if the client does not respond?

Follow up within 3-5 days with a short, friendly ping. A simple "checking in" works better than aggressive follow-ups. Sometimes proposals just get buried.
